# Topic

One of the parts of zerita that are not common in the sentence topic.
The topic is the part of the sentence that is being talked about.
It is easier to understand the topic by examples.

!!! tip "Topic and subject"

    The topic is often the same as the subject. In this case, the
    speaker can choose to use one or the other. As zerita is pro-drop,
    using both isn't common. There is a small difference in meaning,
    when used in a conversation.

    -  _choosing the subject:_ This is neutral, and the carries no special
         meaning.
    -  _choosing the topic:_ This will indicate the speaker's intention to
         talk about the topic.

## Examples

1.  _save de me auti ta coloris azule_

    -   **translation:** As for my car, the color is blue.

1.  _save de me de aute coloris azule_

    -   **translation:** The color of my car is blue.

The first and second sentences both express that the car is blue, but they
are not completely equivalent. The first sentence is more focused on the car,
and the second sentence is more focused on the color. Also, the first sentence
allows the subsequent use of the topic in the rest of the sentence, eg to talk
about the engine, the wheels, etc, without repeating the word car.

1.  _estimevo sulegevo librason. ku vei?_

    -   **translation:** I like reading books. Do you?
    -   **explanation:** Setting the topic of the second sentence to you, instead
        of asking "ku veis?" or "ku estimevis?", indicating that the speaker wants
        to talk about the listener's preferences, as a gentle "I'd rather we focus
        on you now".
