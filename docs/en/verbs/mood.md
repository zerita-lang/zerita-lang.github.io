# Mood

Mood in zerita is expressed by prefixes. The indicative mood has no mood prefix. We split
the the moods in two groups: the major moods which contain the indicative mood,
the subjunctive mood, and the imperative mood, and the minor moods that contain the rest
of the moods.

Major moods are always have `u` in their prefix.

The minor moods are subdivided in two groups: the specific moods, which
are used to express the user's perception of the action, and the general moods,
the general moods, which are used to express something about the action itself, that is
not related to the user's perception.

The general moods are the potential mood and the obligative mood, and contain `o` in their prefix,
and the specific moods are the rest.

## Indicative mood

The indicative mood is used to express facts, and has no mood prefix.

## Subjunctive mood

The subjunctive mood in zerita, similar to the infinite form or the gerund in english, is
used to express the action of a verb, but contrary to them the verb still has a tense and
a person, even another mood. It uses the prefix **su-**, and it's the common mood to be used
in subordinate clauses. When used in a subordinate clause, the verb's tense is relative to
the tense of the main verb.

## Imperative mood

Imperative mood is used to express commands, and uses the mood prefix **vu-**.

## Evidential mood

Evidential mood uses the following mood prefixes:

-   **ve-:** for direct evidence, eg when the speaker has seen the action
-   **ne-:** for hearsay
-   **re-:** for inference

## Dubitative mood

Dubitative mood uses the mood prefix **ab-**. It expresses doubt or scepticism
about the action.

## Optative Mood

The optative mood expresses wishes, desires, or hopes, and uses the mood prefix **fe-**.

## Obligative Mood

The obligative mood expresses necessity or obligation, and uses the mood prefix
**do-**. It's also used to express duty, destiny, or social obligation.

Duty and social obligation use the stabila tense, and destiny uses the simple future.

Negating a verb in the obligative mood means that you don't have the obligation,
rather than meaning you must not perform the action.

There are some subtle differences between using the obligative mood and `oblig` + the verb.
First, `oblig` + verb is the only option when the two verbs are in different tenses, or have
different persons. Second, the obligative mood has a stronger meaning, and it conveys a
meaning of something inescapable.

## Potential Mood

The potential mood expresses the ability or possibility to do something, and uses
the mood prefix **po-**.

As with the obligative mood, there are some subtle differences between using the potential
mood and `pos` + the verb. First, `pos` + verb is the only option when the two verbs are
in different tenses, or have different persons. Second, the potential mood conveys more of
a potential than a permission.

## Examples

1.  _legavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** You are reading the book.

1.  _vulegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** Read the book!

1.  _polegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** You can read the book.

1.  _felegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** May you read the book.

1.  _dolegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** You must read the book.

1.  _obligavis sulegavosis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** You must start reading the book.
    -   **explanation:** As the tense of the verb is different, we can't use the
        obligative mood, but we can use `oblig` + the verb in subjunctive mood.

1.  _velegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** You are reading the book
    -   **explanation:** I have direct evidence about it.

1.  _nelegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** You are reading the book
    -   **explanation:** Someone told me about it.

1.  _relegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** You are reading the book
    -   **explanation:** I inferred that you're doing so, from your other actions.

1.  _ablegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** I don't believe that you're reading the book.

1.  _domortuvur vihomeris._

    -   **translation:** All men must die.

1.  _doservuvur vihomeris._

    -   **translation:** All men must serve.

The moods in zerita can be combined as well:

1.  _abpolegavis ta libron._

    -   **translation:** I doubt that you can read the book.
