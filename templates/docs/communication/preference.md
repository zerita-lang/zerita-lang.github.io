# Preference

Zerita has 4 main verbs to express preference. The verbs are **amor** (love),
**estim** (like), and the negative ones **malamor** (hate) and **malestim**(dislike).
The negation can be used with the positive verbs to express a more nuanced meaning.

The order of likeness is as follows:

- **amor** (love)
- **estim** (like)
- **ne amor** (not love)
- **ne estim** (not like)
- **ne malestim** (not dislike)
- **ne malamor** (not hate)
- **malestim** (dislike)
- **malamor** (hate)

The **ne estim** and **ne malestim** are used to express a neutral feeling, but still
having a slight preference.

The verbs can be used with any person, but outside the first person singular, they
are uncommon unless they are in the evidential mood or in a question.

## Examples

1.  _estimevo sulegevo librason_

    - **translation:** I like reading books.
    - **explanation:** Notice that the the book is in the abstract class, _librason_,
      which means that I like reading books in general, not a specific book.

1.  _ku estimevi sulegevi librason_

    - **translation:** Do you like reading books?

1.  _vestimeve sulegeve librason_

    - **translation:** She likes reading books.
    - **explanation:** I have evidence for that, she may have told me or I may have
      seen her reading a lot.
